Pane is an open-source, AI-native browser that puts the agent **inside** the browser, where your work already lives. Instead of copying URLs and screenshots into a separate chat window, Pane sees your tabs, your logins, and your context directly — and can click, type, read, and reason over them. It can also reach your files and terminal, so a single task can span the web and your machine.

**Open source. Local-first. No servers. The browser for the AI era.**

> Pane is a fork of [BrowserOS](https://github.com/browseros-ai/BrowserOS) — an amazing open-source agentic-browser project. We forked to take a different product trajectory: agent-native, local-first, and server-free.

## Why an agent inside the browser?

Today's AI assistants live *outside* the browser. You paste context into ChatGPT or Claude; context gets lost and workflows break. Standalone agents try to fix this by driving a browser from the outside, through plugins or remote control — but they're always one step removed from your real session.

Pane takes the other path: the agent *is* the browser. Same cookies, tabs, extensions, and logins you already use, plus an agent that can act on them directly. Because it's your browser, it can also learn from what you do and get smarter over time — something a standalone agent simply can't do.

## Quick Start

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Pane">
    Get the latest release from [GitHub releases](https://github.com/abhishek-verma/Pane/releases) for macOS, Windows, or Linux.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import from Chrome">
    Navigate to `chrome://settings/importData` and import your bookmarks, passwords, and settings in one click.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your AI">
    Pane has no default vendor — you choose. Bring your own API keys, sign in with an OAuth subscription, or run models locally.

    <Card title="Set up your LLM provider" icon="key" href="/features/bring-your-own-llm">
      Configure Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, or run models locally with Ollama
    </Card>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try it out">
    Open any webpage and press the **Assistant** button in the toolbar. Ask a question about the page, or describe a task to automate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Which AI model should I use?

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For Chat">
    **Any model works great**, including local LLMs.

    - **Ollama** or **LM Studio** — free, runs locally, great for quick questions
    - **Gemini Flash** — fast and affordable
    - **Claude or GPT-4** — best quality responses

    Chat just needs to understand the page and answer questions — local models handle this well.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For Agent">
    **Use Claude Opus 4.5 for best results.**

    | Model | Speed | Quality |
    |-------|-------|---------|
    | Claude Opus 4.5 | Slower | Best |
    | Claude Sonnet 4.5 | Medium | Great |
    | Claude Haiku 4.5 | Fast | Good |
    | Gemini Flash | Fast | Good |

    <Warning>
    Local LLMs are not yet powerful enough for Agent mode. The model needs strong reasoning to click the right elements and handle multi-step tasks.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Where Pane is heading

Because the agent lives in the browser, Pane can do things no standalone agent can. On the roadmap:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Context Graph" icon="sitemap">
    Pane indexes your browsing, files, terminal, and scheduled work into a local, queryable graph — so it actually knows what you're working on.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Memory & auto-skills" icon="brain">
    Pane remembers you and writes its own skills from workflows you repeat — then prunes what goes stale. It gets smarter the more you use it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Passive capture" icon="video">
    Automatic meeting notes and learnings from your browsing, captured into scoped context buckets — no extra product required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Proactive & reach" icon="bell">
    Triggered runs, a daily digest, and out-of-browser reach (notifications, email, Telegram) so Pane can work for you even when you're away.
  </Card>
</Columns>

See the [specs](https://github.com/abhishek-verma/Pane/tree/main/specs) for the full design.
